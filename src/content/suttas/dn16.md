# DN 16 — Mahāparinibbāna Sutta
*The Great Discourse on the Final Passing*

The Buddha's last journey, last words, and final passing. "Conditioned things are of a nature to decay; strive on with diligence." (vayadhammā saṅkhārā, appamādena sampādetha)

## When to return to this sutta
- [[subcategories/grief_and_loss|Grief & loss]] — Illness, aging, loss of a loved one, facing your own death. The suttas the tradition has always given to the bereaved and the dying.
- [[subcategories/impermanence|Impermanence]] — "Whatever is subject to arising is subject to cessation." The insight that broke stream-enterer after stream-enterer — seen directly, not philosophically.
- [[subcategories/going_forth|The search & awakening]] — The Bodhisatta's own journey — from the going-forth to the awakening to the final nibbāna. The arc the whole path is walking.
