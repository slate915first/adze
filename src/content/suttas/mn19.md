# MN 19 — Dvedhāvitakka Sutta
*Two Kinds of Thought*

The Buddha describes his own pre-awakening practice — sorting his thoughts into two piles: those leading to harm and those leading away from it. The teaching most relevant to the practitioner who feels the work intensify as they advance.

## When to return to this sutta
- [[subcategories/unwanted_thoughts|Unwanted thoughts]] — Rumination, mental proliferation (papañca), thoughts you cannot stop. The Buddha taught specific methods for this — not "let them go" platitudes.
- [[subcategories/effort|Effort & perseverance]] — When you want to give up. When the practice feels stuck. The Bodhisatta's own encounters with Māra — and how he stayed.
