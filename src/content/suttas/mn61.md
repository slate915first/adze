# MN 61 — Ambalaṭṭhikā-rāhulovāda Sutta
*Advice to Rāhula at Ambalaṭṭhikā*

The Buddha to his young son: examine every action of body, speech, and mind before, during, and after — does it lead to the harm of self or others? If yes, do not do it. If no, continue.

## When to return to this sutta
- [[subcategories/speech|How to speak]] — What to say, how to say it, and when silence is kinder. The Buddha was specific: true, beneficial, timely, spoken with a mind of good-will.
- [[subcategories/viewing_progress|How to view progress]] — You cannot see your practice ripen day by day. The suttas that frame progress honestly — and save you from the two pitfalls: thinking you've arrived, and thinking nothing is happening.
