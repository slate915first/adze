# MN 22 — Alagaddūpama Sutta
*The Simile of the Snake*

The dhamma is like a snake — grasped wrongly, it bites. The simile of the raft: even the teaching itself is to be put down once it has carried you across.

## When to return to this sutta
- [[subcategories/wrong_views|Wrong views to guard]] — The views that trap. The ways the dhamma itself can be grasped wrongly — like the snake seized by the tail. Read these before they catch you.
- [[subcategories/not_self|Not-self (anattā)]] — The teaching most likely to be misunderstood. Not "there is no you" — a precise instruction on where to stop identifying, and why it frees.
- [[subcategories/viewing_progress|How to view progress]] — You cannot see your practice ripen day by day. The suttas that frame progress honestly — and save you from the two pitfalls: thinking you've arrived, and thinking nothing is happening.
