# MN 20 — Vitakkasaṇṭhāna Sutta
*The Removal of Distracting Thoughts*

Five methods for handling unwholesome thoughts as they arise: replace with a wholesome counterpart, see their danger, ignore them, examine their root, and as a last resort, suppress with effort of will.

## When to return to this sutta
- [[subcategories/unwanted_thoughts|Unwanted thoughts]] — Rumination, mental proliferation (papañca), thoughts you cannot stop. The Buddha taught specific methods for this — not "let them go" platitudes.
- [[subcategories/hindrances|The five hindrances]] — The specific five things that obstruct concentration and clarity — named, diagnosed, and given antidotes. Required reading for anyone sitting regularly.
