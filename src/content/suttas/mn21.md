# MN 21 — Kakacūpama Sutta
*The Simile of the Saw*

Even if bandits cut you limb from limb with a two-handled saw, you should not allow ill will to arise. The standard the Buddha sets for non-ill-will is the highest in the canon.

## When to return to this sutta
- [[subcategories/anger|Anger & provocation]] — When someone provokes you. When anger arises in yourself. The suttas on meeting hostility without becoming hostile.
- [[subcategories/metta|Loving-kindness]] — Mettā is not a feeling to summon — it is a quality to cultivate, until it becomes boundless. The suttas that frame what that actually means in practice.
