# MN 36 — Mahāsaccaka Sutta
*The Longer Discourse to Saccaka*

The Bodhisatta's years of severe asceticism, his near death, and his remembering the natural calm he experienced as a child under the rose-apple tree. The pivot to the middle way, in his own words.

## When to return to this sutta
- [[subcategories/going_forth|The search & awakening]] — The Bodhisatta's own journey — from the going-forth to the awakening to the final nibbāna. The arc the whole path is walking.
- [[subcategories/effort|Effort & perseverance]] — When you want to give up. When the practice feels stuck. The Bodhisatta's own encounters with Māra — and how he stayed.
