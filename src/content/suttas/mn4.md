# MN 4 — Bhayabherava Sutta
*Fear and Terror*

The Buddha describes his pre-awakening practice of sitting through the night in the forest with fear and dread arising — choosing to remain in whatever posture the fear arose rather than interrupting. The classic sutta on meeting fear in practice.

## When to return to this sutta
- [[subcategories/fear|Fear & dread]] — The Bodhisatta sat through fear in the forest at night. The teachings on what to do when fear arises — and what not to do.
- [[subcategories/effort|Effort & perseverance]] — When you want to give up. When the practice feels stuck. The Bodhisatta's own encounters with Māra — and how he stayed.
