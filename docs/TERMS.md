## 2. What Adze is

Adze is a static web app for keeping a daily meditation practice. It can be used either anonymously (everything stays in your browser's localStorage) or with an account (encrypted sync across devices via Supabase). Source code is public at [github.com/slate915first/adze](https://github.com/slate915first/adze).

## 4. Eligibility

Adze is intended for adults who want a tool to support their own meditation practice. It is not a medical device, not therapy, not a substitute for a teacher or for professional mental health care. If you are in crisis, please use the resources surfaced inside the app and reach out to a person, not the tool.

## 5. Your account

When you receive an invite and create an account, you choose two secrets:

- A **password** for signing in. Resettable by email.
- An **encryption passphrase** that protects your synced practice data. **Not** resettable. If you lose it, your synced data is unrecoverable; the account itself can be reset.

You're responsible for keeping both safe. Don't share them with anyone, including me.

## 6. Acceptable use

Don't use Adze to:

- Attempt to compromise the service, other testers, or yourself.
- Impersonate another person.
- Store content that's illegal where you live.

I reserve the right to suspend or delete an account that's clearly being abused.

## 7. Your data

A separate Privacy Note inside the app (`Settings → Account & sync → How encryption works`) describes exactly what data is stored where, what's encrypted, what isn't, and what your rights are under GDPR / DSGVO. Read it. Short version: anonymous mode = nothing leaves the browser. Synced mode = ciphertext stored on Supabase, encrypted with your passphrase, unreadable by anyone but you.

You can:

- **Export** your data anytime (Settings → Backup & Share → Export).
- **Delete your account** anytime (Settings → Account & sync → Delete account; coming soon, request via email until shipped).
- **Use Adze entirely anonymously** without an account.
